I am Heather Booth

Projects in Digital Composing Portfolio

Phase 1: Exploring Digital Media


Making 1: Projects Fuego

An introduction to who I am as a person, my skills/weaknesses, and aspirations in life. As my first time working with code, I wanted to initiate my “knack” development by challenging myself with different HTML and CSS forms.

Making 2: New Media Webtext Experiment

For my web text experiment, I wanted to incorporate aspects of new media, digital art, and AI. The platform gives users options to explore various interactive/therapeutic art sites. Additionally, to improve user accessibility, I incorporated games for people who are visually impaired.

Making 3: Branching Webtext Design

After reading Plato’s Allegory of the Cave, I was inspired to create a web-text design defining existential crises and the steps to take to get out of one. I have currently been struggling to come to terms with these bigger-picture questions of reality, knowledge, and the meaning of life and was hoping that others (along with myself) could benefit from this type of platform.

Phase 1 Course Goals


I. Foster the creative habits of mind

Although there are still many coding concepts I want to acquire and improve upon, I have learned to be grateful. Not only for the progress I have made, but the privilege of having access to the valuable resources this class provides (and the skills it teaches). Digital composition was a field I knew absolutely nothing about. However, I now know that new media and digital design have essential extensions to many fields (such as philosophy and the sociopolitical sector). I learned that although you can put days/weeks of dedication into a project, it may not always come out as you intended. This curriculum makes me apply multidisciplinary skills in a way I never have- I appreciate this challenge.

II. Engage with others in a workshop environment

I have a fear of public speaking, and although our class is extremely small, putting my work “out there” for people to critique was nerve-racking. Collaborating with my peers allowed me to recognize my flaws/weaknesses in design (such as using more reader-friendly fonts) and helped me diminish my anxiety when presenting. For me, it was extremely insightful to work in a community-based collaborative environment. My group was able to help one another “tweak” elements that weren't working correctly, give each other encouragement, and offer new ideas that weren’t previously thought of. I think it's important to think of one another as a tool. If we combine our knowledge and experience I’m thinking we can produce some pretty incredible things. Although my knowledge in this field is quite limited, I'm looking forward to helping others to the best of my ability.

III. Think deeply about craft

I completely scrapped my original Branching Webtext Design project, not only because my theme was loosely connected to the readings, but because I realized it didn’t reflect the substantial amount of time I spent thinking about the concepts from this class. I was so focused on framing my projects around some general final project theme that I lost touch with the new creative outlooks I had developed. I definitely need to begin finalizing a definitive goal for my project and formulating a distinctive/objective craft (it’s difficult for me because this class makes me question larger/universal concepts). I might focus on the ability of media to incite emotions such as fear in a way that's distant from human nature. I also think it's really interesting that we can combine computer science (concrete elements) and intangible elements (those without limits/bounds)- a concept I plan to “toy” with in future projects.

IV. Engage in sustained research and skills development

The progression of these three projects allowed me to continuously review my work and acquire perseverance (a new-found dedication to developing my craft). For my branching web text design, I needed to conduct research not only to develop a comprehensive understanding of what an existential crisis actually is, but recognize how to communicate this concept to others. Reading blogs about people’s personal struggles with identity allowed me to connect with their stories and prompted me to use more of an interactive approach for the setup of my site.

V. Learn to present your work

My praxis was to explore the diverse and limitless bounds of digital composition and construct projects that express my creativity. I aimed to design sites that would visually draw the user in without being overwhelming. Specifically, I wanted to focus on finding a balance between creativity/ accessibility while connecting material from class to a subject matter relevant to my personal life. Living in this post Covid era has been difficult for many people, and I feel as though we as a society tend to ignore/filter out our feelings, emotions, or questions about life as a whole. My branching design was a therapeutic, learning process that I created to help others know they are not alone.
